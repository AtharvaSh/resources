Chapter : Chemical Reactions & Equations

Chemical Reaction

Whenever a chemical change occur , we can say that a chemical reaction has taken place.

  • Change in state
  • Change in color
  • Evolution of a gas
  • Change in temperature

Chemical equations can be written in the form of :

  • Word Equation : Eg.= Magnesium + Oxygen = Magnesium oxide
  • Letter Equation: Eg. Mg + O2

TYPES OF CHEMICAL REACTIONS

  1. Exothermic Reaction :
    A reaction in which heat is released from an reaction called exothermic reaction.
  2. Endothermic Reaction:
    A reaction in which heat is absorbed from our surrounding known as endothermic reaction.
  3. Combination Reaction:
    A reaction in which a single product is formed from two or more
    reactants called Combination Reaction .

    4.  Eg: CaO (s) + H2O(l) ===> Ca[OH]2 (aq) + Heat
    # : 2H2(g) + OSUB>2(g) ===> 2H2O(l)
  4. Decomposition Reaction:
    A reaction in which two or more products
    are formed from one reactant is called decomposition Reaction.
     Eg: 2Pb(NO3)2(s) + Heat ===> 2PbO(S) + 4NO2(g) +O2
    #: 2AgCl(s) + Sunlight ===> 2Ag(s) + Cl2(g)

    Types of Decomposition Reaction

    • Thermal Decomposition Reaction: A reaction in which heat is given to the reaction for breakdown of reactants.
       CaCO3(S) + Heat ===> Cao(s) + CO2(g)
    • Electrolytic / Electrolysis Decomposition Reaction: A reaction in which electricity is given to the reaction for breakdown of reactants.
       2H2O(l) + Electricity ===> 2H2(l) + O2(g)
    • Photolytic/Photolysis Decomposition Reaction: A reaction in which light or sunlight is given to the reaction for breakdown of reactants.
       2AgCl(s) + Sunlight ===> 2Ag(s) + Cl2(g)
       2AgBr(s) + Sunlight ===> 2Ag(s) + Br2(g)
  5. Displacement Reaction
    A reaction in which more reactive element reactive element
    displace the less reactive element and formed new product.
    Eg: Fe(s) CuSO4(aq) + ===> + FeSO4(aq) + Cu(s)
     Eg: Zn(s) CuSO4(aq) + ===> + ZnSO4(aq) + Cu(s)
     Eg: Pb(s) CuCl2(aq) + ===> + PbCl2(aq) + Cu(s)
  6. Double Displacement Reaction
    A reaction in which exchange of anion and cation take place and formed new product
    and also form an aquous solution.
    Eg: Na2SO4(aq) + BaCl42(aq) + ===> + BaSO4(s) + 2NaCl(aq)
  7. Redox Reaction
    A reaction in which oxidation and reduction take place called as Redox Reaction
    Eg: CuO + H2 ===> Cu(REDUCTION) + H2 O(OXIDATION)
    1. Oxidation Reaction: A reaction in which gain of oxygen or lose of hydrogen take place then the reaction called as Oxidation Reaction.
      2. 2Cu + O2 + Heat ===> 2CuO
    2. Reduction Reaction: A reaction in which lose of oxygen or gain of hydrogen take place then the reaction called as Reduction Reaction.
      3. CuO + H2 + Heat ===> Cu + H2O
