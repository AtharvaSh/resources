Parts of human eye

Cornea

It is the outermost part of human eue . It protects eye from hostile elements and refracts 70-80% of light.

Aqueous humour

It maintains pressure inside and outside eye.

Iris

It is a black muscular diaphragm which controls size of pupil.

Pupil

It regulates amount of light entering the eye.

Crystalline eye lens

It is a flexible convex lens that converges light on retina.

Ciliary muscles

It adjusts focal length of crystalline eye lens.

Vitreous Humour

It maintains shape of eye.

Retina

  1. It is a screen made up of photosensitive cells on which image is formed.
  2. It contains two type of cells.
    • Rod cells- Its fuction is to sense intensity of light.
    • Cone cells- Its function is to sense colour of light.

Optic Nerve

It converts light into electrical signals and sends to brain.

Power of accomodation

It is the ability of eye lens to adjust its focal length as per the distances by the ciloary muscles.

Defects in human eye

Myopia

  • It is a kind of eye defect in which a person is able to see nearby objects clearly but cannot see farther objects clearly.
  • Its causes are
    1. Excessive curvature of eye lens
    2. Elongation of eye ball
  • It is correted by concave lens.

Hypermetropia

  • It is a kind of eye defect in which a person is able to see farther objects clearly but cannot see nearby objects clearly.
  • It is caused by
    1. Decrease in power of eye lens.
    2. Shortening of eye lens.
  • It is corrected by using convex lens.

Presbyopia

  • It is a eye defect which is caused due to agening.
  • Its causes are
    1. Decrese in flexibility of eye lens.
    2. Gradual weakeing of eye lens

Some important terms

Least distance of distict vision

  • It is a minimum distance at which objects can be seen most distinctly without strain.
  • It is 25cm in a normal human eye.

Bifocal lens

  • It is a lens which is used by a person suffering from both myopia and heteropia.
  • Upper portion of eye lens is made up of concave lens and lower part is made up of conceve lens.

Refraction of monochromatic light

Dispersion of light

  • The phenomenon of splitting of white light into its seven constituents colours when passed through a prism.

Rainbow

  • water droplets acts like a prism.
  • Water droplets refracts and disperse incident sunlight, then reflect it internally and refract it again when it comes out of raindrop.
  • Due to dispersion and internal reflection, different colours reach our eye.
  • Red colour appears on top and and bluolour on bottom.
  • Rainbow is always formed in opposite direction of sun.

Atmospheric Refraction

The refraction of light by earth's atmosphere because different layers of atmosphere have different optical density.

Scattering of light

Light is absorbed by particles and then scattered in all direction.

Tyndall Effect

When a beam of light strikes minute particles of earth's atmosphere then the path of light becomes visible dur to scattering of light.

Twinkling of stars

  • Starlight on entering earth's atmosphere undergoes refraction continuously before reaching our eye.
  • Since stars are distant , they are poit sized source of light.
