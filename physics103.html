Electriciy

Electricity is flow of charge.

Eletric charge

Electric charge is the invariant property of matter due to which it experiences or expresses electrical and magnetic effect.

Electric current

  • Electric current is the flow of charge from a cross section per unit time.
  • Its SI unit is Ampere.
  • I=Q/t

Potential difference or voltage

  • Potential differene is the amount of work done to move a unit charge from a potential point to another potential point.
  • Voltage=Amount of work done/Charge
  • Its SI unit is Volt.

Ohm's law

  • Ohm law states that voltage across ends of a conductor os directly proportional to amount of current flowing through it.
  • V∝I V=IR

Resistance

  • Resistance is the opposition experienced by current due to atomic particles.
  • Factors affecting resistance
    1. Length of the conductor(R∝l)
    2. Area of cross section(R∝1/l)
    3. Material of wire(ρ)
    4. Temperature

Combination of resistors

There are 2 types of resistors

  1. Series
  2. Parallel

Series

  • In series circuit current remains same throughout the circuit but the voltage is divided between all resistors.

Parallel circuit

  • In parallel circuit voltage remains same throughout all resistors but the current changes.

Advantages of parallel circuit over series circuit

  1. Every device gets same voltage so operates on same speed.
  2. Any device is not affected by failure of other device like in series circuit.
  3. Reduces the overall resistance that results in less current consumption,
