Magnetic effects of electric current

Oersted experiment

  • When oersted brought a magnetic compass near a current carrying conductot it showed deflection.
  • This gave conclusion that there is a relation between magnet and current.

Magnetic field

  • Magnetic field is the area around a magnet in ehich agnetic force can be experienced be any other magnet , ferromagnetic materials or ions.

Magnetic field lines

    Magnetic field lines are lines that denote magnetic field of any object.

Properties of magnetic field lines

  1. Magnetic field lines emerge from north pole and merge at south pole .
  2. Inside the magnet they run from south to north
  3. Magnetic field lines are closed curves.
  4. Magnetic field lines do not intersect esch other.

Why magnetic field lines do not intersect each other

  1. If magnetic field lines will intersect each other at point , the magnetic compass will show two different directions at the same time. That is not possible.

Current carrying conductor

Maxwell's right hand thumb rule

  • Maxwell's right hand thumb rule states that if we grasp a current carrying conductor in our hand and our thumb is in the diection of current then the direction in which our fingers encircle the wire is the direction of magnetic field around it.

Current carrying loop

  • In a current carrying loop, if current flows in clockwise direction then south pole is formed
  • If current flows in anti-clockwise directon then north pole is formed.

Fleming's left hand rule

  • Fleming's left hand rule states that if a current carrying force is kept in a magnetic field then the force experienced by the conductor is mutually perpendicular to the direction of magnetic field and direction of current.
